Empire Stout :: May 17th 2008

So named because I live on Empire Place.

O.G. 1.060
F.G. 1.024
ABV 4.65%
203 Calories / 12 oz.

Ingredients for 5 gallons
  • 1 lb. Black Patent Malt (crushed)
  • 1 lb. British Caramel Malt (crushed)
  • 1/3 lb. 450°L British Crisp Chocolate Malt (crushed)
  • 6.6 lb. Cooper's Light Malt Extract Syrup
  • 3.3 lb. Cooper's Dark Malt Extract Syrup
  • 2.0 oz. Domestic Hallertaur Hops, fresh whole leaf (3.6% aa)
  • 1 vial White Labs live yeast culture - English Ale WLP002
  • 3/4 cup priming sugar (bottling)

Procedure
Add the grains to 1 gallon water. Bring this mixture up to 150°, stirring regularly. Remove from heat and maintain 150° (or as close as possible) for at least 10 minutes. Return to full heat and bring wort to a boil.
When mixture begins to boil, strain the mixture into the brewpot, removing spent grains. Add 2 gallons of cold water to the brewpot and return to heat.
As wort nears boil, add the malt extract. When wort returns to boil, add 1 oz. hallertaur hops. Boil all this for one hour at rolling boil.
Add remaining hops for the last 2 minutes of the boil, and remove from heat. Run wort chiller for 15 minutes and combine wort with 3 gallons cold water in the 6 gallon fermenter.
Pitch the yeast and affix the airlock.

Tasting Notes
This beer stayed too long in the secondary because I was procrastinating bottling it. I believe the yeast began to lose vigor. The result was beer that didn't bottle ferment very well and will go flat in just a short time. This beer is otherwise unremarkable, not my best effort.
