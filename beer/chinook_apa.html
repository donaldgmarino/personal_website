Chinook American Pale Ale :: December 16, 2007

Hops, Hops, Hops.

O.G. 1.043
F.G. 1.012
ABV 4.02%
143 Calories / 12 oz.

Ingredients for 5 gallons
  • 7 lb. Cooper's Light Malt Extract Liquid
  • 1.0 lb. 40°L Crystal Malt (crushed)
  • 1/3 lb. Castle Belgian Biscuit Malt
  • 2.0 oz. Chinook Hops, fresh whole leaf (14.4% aa)
  • 2.0 oz. Cascade Hops, fresh whole leaf (6.0% aa)
  • 2.0 oz. Centennial Hop pellets (9.3% aa)
  • 2.0 tsp. Gypsum
  • 2 tsp. Irish Moss
  • 1 vial White Labs live yeast culture - California Ale WLP001
  • 3/4 cup priming sugar (bottling)

Procedure
Steep grains in ~1 gallon water, bringing up tp 150°. Steep at 150° for 20 minutes.
Strain wort into 1.5 gallons hot water in the brewpot.
Bring the wort to a boil, then add the gypsum, Irish Moss, and the malt extract.
When the wort returns to a boil, add the Chinook hops and proceed with rolling boil.
With 2 minutes left in the boil, add all of the Cascade hops and the Centennial pellets.
Run wort chiller for 20 minutes, leaving the hops in the wort.
Remove hops with sanitized strainer, then combine wort with 3 gallons cold water in the fermenter.
Pitch yeast immediately.
Racked into clean secondary after initial fermentation.
Bottle with 3/4 cup primimg sugar as usual.

Tasting Notes
Some of this beer was very good, while I had an infection on at least half of the bottles. The uninfected samples of this beer were very close to what I'd expected. A very hoppy, medium/light bodied Pale Ale with pronounced hop nose, this was quite good and drinkable. I was disappointed to have my first skunky beer since resuming the brew-works last June, but I suppose it's inevitable to have some problems sometime in home brewing. At least the recipe was solid. I will continue to refine the American Pale Ale recipe, this was my third such beer, and I've since brewed another version of the APA.
