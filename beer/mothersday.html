Mother's Day Ale :: May 9th 2010

Brewed on Mother's Day 2010.

O.G. 1.035
F.G. 1.010
ABV 3.25%

Ingredients for 5 gallons
  • 1 lb. Belgian Biscuit Malt (crushed)
  • 1 lb. British Caramel Malt (crushed)
  • 7.5 lb. Cooper's Light Malt Extract Syrup
  • 4.0 oz. Liberty Hops, fresh whole leaf (3.6% aa)
  • 1 vial White Labs live yeast culture - California V Ale WLP051

Procedure
Put the crushed malt into a boiling bag and add the grains to 1 gallon water. Bring wort to a boil. Remove grain bag, draining as much liquor as possible one the mixture reaches a boil.
As wort begins to boil, add the malt extract. When wort returns to boil, add 2.0 oz Liberty hops. Boil all this for one hour at rolling boil.
Add remaining 2.0 oz hops for the last 15 minutes of the boil, and remove from heat. 
Run wort chiller for 15 minutes and combine wort with 3 gallons cold water in the fermenter.
Pitch the yeast at room temperature and affix the airlock.

Tasting Notes
Kegged this batch for the first time. Had left this in both the primary and the secondary for a long time, a few weeks apiece. Beer was good, clean tasting and very drinkable. I wanted this beer to be lighter, but this was a little too light. Needed more hops, too. Would want to improve this recipe if I made it again but was pleased with the kegging results. Good, drinkable session ale.
