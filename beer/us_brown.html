American Brown Ale :: December 6, 2007

Adapted from a recipe I saw that was supposed to be close to Brooklyn Brown Ale.

O.G. 1.051
F.G. 1.016
ABV 4.53%
170 Calories / 12 oz.

Ingredients for 5 gallons
  • 1 lb. 60°L Crystal Malt
  • 6 oz. 450°L British Crisp Chocolate Malt
  • 4 oz. Castle Belgian Biscuit Malt
  • 7.5 lb. Cooper's Light Malt Extract Syrup
  • 2.0 oz. Cascade Hops, fresh whole leaf (6.0% aa)
  • 2.0 oz. Northern Brewer Hops, fresh whole leaf (8.5% aa)
  • 1 tsp. Irish Moss
  • 2 oz. Malto-Dextrin powder
  • 1.0 tsp. Gypsum
  • 1 vial White Labs live yeast culture - London Ale WLP013
  • 3/4 cup priming sugar (bottling)

Procedure
Steep specialty grains in 1 gallon water. Raise water to 150° and remove from heat. Keep as close to 150° as possible for 30 minutes.
After 30 minutes, strain this mixture into the brewpot, through a strainer, following with 1 gallon 130° water to simulate sparging. Add more hot water to brewpot to total 2 gallons.
Bring this to a boil, and add the malt extract.
When the wort returns to a boil, add the Malto-dextrin, and 1.25 oz. Northern Brewer hops.
Boil for 30 minutes, then add the Irish Moss and an additional 3/4 oz. Northern Brewer hops.
Boil for 15 more minutes, then add 3/4 oz. Cascade hops.
Boil another 15 minutes, then add the remaining 1.25 oz. or so of Cascade hops.
Only boil for 2 more minutes, then remove from heat.
Run wort chiller for 20 minutes, leaving the hops in the wort.
Remove hops with clean strainer, then combine wort with 3 gallons cold water in the fermenter.
Pitch yeast into cooled wort.
Racked into clean 5 gallon carboy after 9 days in the primary. Continue to ferment in the secondary for another week.
Bottle with the usual 3/4 cup priming sugar.

Tasting Notes
This beer turned out beautifully; a nice dark brown, well-balanced beer, with a decent amount of hop nose. I thought this was one of my more successful efforts and was quite reminiscent of a Pete's Wicked Ale. Very drinkable and an encouraging sign of progress in the home brewery!
