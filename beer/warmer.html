Winter Warmer 2008 :: Dec. 1, 2007

I wanted to make a seasonal beer to give away to folks for Christmas gifts this year. I decided to try a Winter Warmer. This beer ended up at 6.75% ABV! That ought to warm you up.

O.G. 1.079
F.G. 1.027
ABV 6.75%
267 Calories / 12 oz.

Ingredients for 5 gallons
  • 3 lb. Munton's Plain Amber Dried Malt Extract
  • 6 lb. Munton's Plain Light Dried Malt Extract
  • 4 oz. Willamette Hops (whole fresh) 4.4% alpha
  • 1 lb. 40°L Crystal Malt
  • 1/2 oz. British Crisp Chocolate Malt
  • 1.0 tsp. Gypsum
  • 1 Vial White Labs London Ale Yeast Live Culture (LWP013)
  • 3/4 cup priming sugar (bottling)

Procedure
Bring 2 gallons of water up tp 150° while steeping the cracked grains. When mixture reaches 150°, lower heat and keep at 150° for 20 minutes., then continue to heat to a boil.
When the wort begins to boil, remove grain bags, and add the malt extract.
When wort returns to a boil, add 3 ounces of the Willamette hops, and boil at a rolling boil for one hour.
For the last 3 minutes of the boil, add the remaining Willamette hops.
Remove from heat, run wort chiller for 20 minutes, then combine with 3 gallons of cold water in the fermenter.
Pitch yeast immediately into well-aerated wort.
Bottle with 3/4 cup primimg sugar when fermentation complete.

Tasting Notes
I never got to taste this beer, sonce I gave it all away. People gave it pretty favorable reviews, however, so it couldn't have been too bad. Hopefully it was actually good and folks weren't just being polite. :-)
